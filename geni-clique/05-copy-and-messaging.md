# 05 — Copy & Messaging System

## 1. Voice

Geni Clique writes like a senior engineer with taste explaining something valuable to a busy CEO. Calm. Concrete. Slightly dry wit permitted; enthusiasm earned by evidence.

**Principles**
1. **Outcome first, mechanism second.** "31 hours back every week" before "multi-agent orchestration."
2. **Short declaratives carry weight.** The brand's rhythm is the full stop. *"The work runs itself."*
3. **Numbers are the adjectives.** Where a competitor writes "blazing fast," we write "4 minutes per run." No number? Cut the claim.
4. **Name the pain plainly.** "Chasing invoices" beats "inefficient AR workflows."
5. **Quiet confidence, no dares.** We never trash competitors, never overclaim, never exclaim. Zero exclamation marks sitewide.
6. **Human, not casual.** Contractions yes; slang, memes, "hey there 👋" — no.

**Banned list** (auto-fail in review): revolutionize, unleash, unlock, supercharge, turbocharge, 10x, game-changing, cutting-edge, next-gen, seamless, synergy, empower (as filler), "AI-powered future", "digital transformation journey", any emoji in body copy.

## 2. Messaging hierarchy

| Level | Message |
|---|---|
| One-liner | We build AI systems that do your repetitive work. |
| Elevator | Geni Clique is an AI systems studio. We design and build agents, automations, and custom software that take repetitive work off your team — so the hours go back into the work that actually grows the business. |
| Category label | AI systems studio *(not "agency" — studios build; agencies talk)* |
| Proof themes | Hours returned · Revenue response speed · Cost per task · Reliability ("boringly reliable") |

## 3. Headline bank

**Hero candidates** (test order): The work runs itself. · Intelligence, employed. · Busywork, retired. · Your business, running while you sleep.

**Section-heading formulas**
- *Noun, verbed:* "Mondays, returned." "Busywork, retired."
- *Quiet claim + receipt:* "Weeks, not quarters." "Proof, not promises."
- *Plain question the buyer has:* "Will it work with our stack?" (then answer immediately)

## 6. CTA strategy

One conversion action sitewide: **the strategy call.** Every CTA routes there. Secondary CTAs only ever deepen engagement (see work, read the build) — never split conversion across "newsletter / demo / pricing / contact" competing asks. Email fallback (mailto) accompanies the final CTA for the allergic-to-calendars executive.

## 7. Tone calibration by section

| Section | Temperature |
|---|---|
| Hero, manifesto | Cinematic — biggest claims, fewest words |
| Services, ecosystem | Precise — technical nouns allowed, always translated |
| Case studies | Journalistic — problem, build, number; zero adjectives |
| Why / Process | Warmest register — this is where the humans show |
| FAQ | Straightest register — answer first, no marketing |
| Footer/legal | Dry wit permitted, one wink maximum |
